# Design Archetype: Friendly Illustrated

## Atmosphere & Mood

A warm, approachable design language that feels like a conversation with a helpful friend. The aesthetic is driven by colorful but soft illustrations, rounded shapes, and a palette that feels optimistic without being overwhelming. This is the visual language of collaborative tools, consumer apps, and educational platforms.

The design should feel tactile — surfaces are soft, corners are rounded, and interactions are bouncy. Photography is warm and human-centric. Iconography is playful and often hand-drawn or organic in style.

**Key Characteristics:**
- Warm, approachable color palette with multiple soft accents
- Rounded corners on almost everything (cards, buttons, images, inputs)
- Illustration-first: organic, hand-drawn, or flat-color vector art
- Soft, warm photography featuring diverse people and real environments
- Generous whitespace but not stark minimalism — content feels nestled, not floating
- Friendly, conversational typography
- Bouncy micro-interactions (hover lifts, soft shadows)

## Color Philosophy

### Primary Roles
- **Primary surface**: Warm white or very light cream. Never cold or sterile.
- **Elevated surface**: Pure white or slightly lighter warm white for cards and containers.
- **Primary text**: Warm near-black or very dark brown-gray.
- **Secondary text**: Warm medium gray.
- **Brand accent**: A single friendly primary color (warm coral, soft blue, approachable green, or playful purple).
- **Illustration palette**: 3–5 soft, harmonious colors used consistently across all illustrations.

### Surface & Border Logic
- Borders are warm and barely visible, or replaced entirely by soft shadows.
- Dark sections (if used) should be deep but warm — deep charcoal with brown undertones, never pure black.

### Gradient Strategy
- Soft, warm gradients are welcome for hero backgrounds or card accents.
- Avoid harsh neon or high-contrast gradients.

## Typography Direction

### Genre
- **Headlines**: Friendly, rounded sans-serif. Medium to bold weight.
- **Body**: Clean, readable sans-serif. Regular weight.
- **Accent / Labels**: Same sans-serif, medium weight, often in the brand accent color.

### Principles
- Generous line-height for body text (1.6+) for a relaxed reading experience.
- Headlines should feel friendly, not corporate.
- Avoid overly tight letter-spacing.

## Component Patterns

### Buttons
- **Primary**: Brand accent background with white text. Generous padding, rounded corners (12–16px). Soft shadow.
- **Secondary**: White background with brand accent border and text.
- **Soft / Tertiary**: Light tinted background with matching text. No border.

### Cards
- Background: elevated surface.
- Radius: very rounded (16–24px).
- Shadow: soft, warm drop shadow.
- Often includes an illustration at the top.

### Inputs
- Background: white or light tint.
- Border: thin warm gray.
- Focus: brand accent ring.
- Radius: rounded (8–12px).

### Navigation
- Warm, clean header. Logo + friendly links.
- CTA: primary accent button.
- Mobile: hamburger or bottom navigation.

### Illustrations & Icons
- Consistent style across all illustrations (flat, organic, or 3D).
- Use the illustration palette for all art.
- Icons should match the illustration style (rounded, friendly).

## Layout & Spacing

### Spacing
- Base unit: 8px.
- Generous padding inside cards (24–32px).
- Large section gaps (80–120px).

### Grid & Container
- Max width: 1200–1400px.
- Feature grids: 2–4 columns.
- Hero: often split between text and illustration.

## Depth & Elevation

| Level | Treatment | Use |
|-------|-----------|-----|
| Flat | No shadow | Backgrounds |
| Soft | Subtle warm drop shadow | Cards, buttons |
| Floating | Larger shadow + lift on hover | Interactive cards |

## Do's and Don'ts

### Do
- Use rounded corners everywhere.
- Use warm, soft colors.
- Use friendly illustrations and photography.
- Make interactions feel bouncy and responsive.
- Maintain consistency in illustration style.

### Don't
- Don't use sharp corners.
- Don't use cold, corporate colors.
- Don't use generic stock photography.
- Don't make the interface feel sterile or overly minimal.

## Agent Prompt Guide

### Example Semantic Prompts
- "Create a hero section with a friendly illustration, warm cream background, and rounded primary CTA in coral."
- "Design a feature grid with illustrated cards, generous radius, and soft shadows."
- "Build a sign-up form with rounded inputs, warm borders, and a friendly accent button."
