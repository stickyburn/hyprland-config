# Design Archetype: Playful Gradient

## Atmosphere & Mood

A bold, motion-first design language where gradients are not decoration — they are the identity. The interface feels alive, dynamic, and creative. This archetype suits design tools, creative portfolios, prototyping apps, and any product that wants to express innovation and artistic freedom.

Gradients flow across backgrounds, text, buttons, and borders. The palette is vivid but controlled, usually anchored by a deep dark or bright white that allows the gradients to pop. Typography is confident and modern, often geometric.

**Key Characteristics:**
- Multi-color gradients as primary identity element
- Bold, large typography with high contrast
- Dark mode or bright mode backgrounds that make gradients sing
- Motion-first thinking: elements feel like they are transitioning or morphing
- Generous border-radius and soft shadows
- Minimal chrome; content and gradient are the focus
- Geometric or abstract shapes as decorative elements

## Color Philosophy

### Primary Roles
- **Primary surface**: Deep dark (navy, charcoal, or near-black) OR clean bright white. The goal is maximum contrast with gradients.
- **Primary text**: White on dark surfaces; near-black on light surfaces.
- **Gradient anchor**: A set of 2–4 colors that form the brand gradient.
- **Accent**: One of the gradient colors used as a solid for buttons and links.

### Gradient Usage
- Hero backgrounds: full-bleed animated or static gradients.
- Text: gradient text fills for headlines (using `background-clip: text`).
- Buttons: gradient backgrounds with white text.
- Borders: gradient borders on cards or featured sections.
- Decorative shapes: blurred gradient blobs or orbs in the background.

## Typography Direction

### Genre
- **Headlines**: Bold geometric sans-serif. Large, commanding.
- **Body**: Clean geometric sans, regular weight.
- **Labels**: Medium weight, often uppercase with wide letter-spacing.

### Principles
- High contrast between text and background.
- Gradients on headlines are a signature move.
- Generous sizing for display text.

## Component Patterns

### Buttons
- **Gradient primary**: Multi-color gradient background, white text, generous radius.
- **Outline gradient**: Gradient border with transparent or dark fill.
- **Solid accent**: One gradient color as solid fill for secondary actions.

### Cards
- Background: dark or white.
- Gradient top or left border for featured cards.
- Soft shadow.
- Radius: generous (16–24px).

### Navigation
- Minimal. Gradient accent on active link or CTA.
- Dark nav bar with gradient logo or CTA.

## Layout & Spacing

- Full-width sections with gradient backgrounds.
- Large, dramatic spacing between sections.
- Asymmetric layouts welcome.

## Depth & Elevation

| Level | Treatment | Use |
|-------|-----------|-----|
| Flat | No shadow | Gradient backgrounds |
| Soft | Subtle shadow | Cards on gradient |
| Glow | Colored shadow matching gradient | Featured elements |

## Do's and Don'ts

### Do
- Use gradients boldly and consistently.
- Use dark or white backgrounds to make gradients pop.
- Animate gradients subtly if possible.
- Use gradient text for headlines.

### Don't
- Don't use muted gradients — they should be vivid.
- Don't clash gradient colors with the background.
- Don't overuse gradients on every element — let them be accents.

## Agent Prompt Guide

### Example Semantic Prompts
- "Create a hero section with a full-bleed animated gradient (purple to pink to orange) on a dark background. Bold geometric sans headline in gradient text fill."
- "Design a feature card with a gradient top border, dark background, and soft shadow."
- "Build a CTA section with a gradient button and gradient decorative blobs in the background."
